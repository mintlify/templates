Your first day is all about getting oriented. Follow the checklist in order.

## Before you start

Your manager sent a welcome email with:

- A link to activate your `@mintlify.com` Google Workspace account
- Your start date and first-day schedule
- A Slack invite

If any of these are missing, contact your recruiter or Han/Hahnbee directly before your start date.

## Checklist

1. **Activate your company email** — Log in to [mail.google.com](https://mail.google.com) with your `@mintlify.com` address. Set a strong password and enable two-factor authentication immediately.
2. **Set up your laptop** — Follow the [laptop setup guide](/onboarding/tools/laptop-setup). Laptops ship with MDM pre-installed, but you'll need to complete enrollment and install role-specific tools.
3. **Join Slack** — Your Slack invite arrives in your Mintlify email. Download the desktop app and post a hello in `#introductions`.
4. **Claim your accounts** — Log in to GitHub, Linear, Notion, and Figma using your `@mintlify.com` email. See [account access](/onboarding/tools/account-access) for the full list.
5. **Join key Slack channels** — At minimum: `#general`, `#eng`, `#product`, and `#random`. Your manager will point you to team-specific channels.
6. **Meet your onboarding buddy** — You've been paired with a teammate who'll answer questions and show you around. They'll reach out on Slack — send them a message first if you'd like.
7. **1:1 with your manager** — This is scheduled for your first day. Come with questions. They'll walk you through your first 30 days and who you should meet.

## People to meet today

| Person | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Your manager | Walk through your first 30 days and team context |
| Your onboarding buddy | Day-to-day questions and informal orientation |
| Your immediate teammates | The people you'll work with most closely |

## What's normal on day one

You will not ship anything on your first day — that's expected. The goal is orientation. If you feel overwhelmed by the number of tools and channels, that's completely normal.

If something in your setup is broken or you can't access a tool, post in `#general` and someone will help you.
