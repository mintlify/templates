Your first week is about building context, not shipping. Measure yourself by how much you understand by Friday, not by how much you've shipped.

## Goals for week one

By end of week one you should:

- Have all accounts active and your local dev environment running
- Know who you'll work with most and how the team operates
- Understand what Mintlify does and who our customers are
- Have a clear project or task to focus on in week two
- Have made at least one small contribution — a docs fix, a code review, a bug investigation

## Recommended daily focus

**Day 1** — Setup and orientation. Follow the [first day checklist](/onboarding/first-day). Get every account working. Meet your manager and onboarding buddy.

**Day 2–3** — Product immersion. Spend a morning using Mintlify as a customer: create a project, deploy a docs site, make edits in the web editor, open a pull request. Read [mintlify.com/docs](https://mintlify.com/docs) end to end. Book 20-minute intros with each direct teammate — ask what they're working on and what slows them down.

**Day 4** — Codebase and architecture. Follow the [dev setup guide](/engineering/dev-setup) if you haven't already. Read [services overview](/engineering/architecture/services-overview) and [data models](/engineering/architecture/data-models). Ask your tech lead for a codebase walkthrough.

**Day 5** — Reflection and first contribution. Write down your three biggest open questions and share them with your manager. Make your first small contribution — fix a typo, leave a code review comment, or close a starter issue.

## Asking for help

At Mintlify we prefer asking questions early rather than spinning solo. The `#eng` channel is the right place for most engineering questions. Your onboarding buddy is there for anything — no question is too basic in your first week.

If your manager asks how things are going and you say "fine" when they aren't, you're not helping yourself. Be honest about blockers.
