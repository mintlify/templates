title: "Phishing awareness"
description: "How to spot phishing attempts and what to do when you receive a suspicious message."
---

Phishing is the most common vector for account compromise. Knowing the patterns makes them easy to spot before you click anything.

## Common patterns

**Urgency and pressure** — "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours." Legitimate systems don't work like this. When in doubt, verify through a separate channel (Slack, not a reply to the email).

**Mismatched sender domains** — An email claiming to be from GitHub or Stripe with a domain like `github-security-alert.com` is a red flag. Check the full email address, not just the display name.

**Requests for credentials** — No legitimate Mintlify system will ask you to enter your password via an email link. If a link takes you to an unexpected login page, close it.

**Unexpected attachments** — Don't open attachments you weren't expecting, especially `.exe`, `.zip`, or macro-enabled Office files.

## What to do

**If you receive a suspicious email:**
1. Don't click links or download attachments.
2. Forward it to [security@mintlify.com](mailto:security@mintlify.com) and post in `#general` so others are aware.
3. Delete it.

**If you clicked a link or entered credentials:**
1. Change your password immediately and revoke any active sessions.
2. Notify the team in `#general` — include the URL you visited.
3. Don't wait to see if anything happens. Speed matters in a credential compromise.

## Simulated phishing tests

We run periodic simulated phishing campaigns. Clicking a simulated link triggers a short training module — it's not punitive. The goal is awareness.

If you notice an unusual volume of phishing attempts targeting your Mintlify address specifically, notify the team in `#general`.
