---
title: "Code of conduct"
description: "How everyone at Mintlify is expected to treat each other and our customers."
---

We hold ourselves to a simple standard: treat everyone with respect, act with integrity, and take responsibility for your actions.

## Our expectations

**Respectful communication** — Disagree with ideas, not people. Criticism is welcome and necessary — personal attacks are not. This applies in Slack, in code reviews, in meetings, and everywhere else we interact.

**Inclusion** — We don't tolerate discrimination or harassment on the basis of race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability, national origin, or any other protected characteristic. We also don't tolerate exclusionary jokes or comments that make colleagues feel unwelcome.

**Integrity** — Be honest with colleagues, managers, and customers. If you make a mistake, own it and fix it. Don't cover up errors or shift blame.

**Confidentiality** — Respect the confidentiality of Mintlify's information and customer data. Don't share internal information with people who don't need to know.

**Conflicts of interest** — Disclose any situation where your personal interests could conflict with Mintlify's. When in doubt, ask People or a founder.

## What counts as harassment

Harassment includes (but is not limited to):

- Unwelcome comments about someone's appearance, identity, or personal life
- Persistent unwanted contact after someone has asked you to stop
- Sharing someone's private information without their consent
- Creating or forwarding material that demeans colleagues

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code:

1. If you're comfortable, tell the person directly that their behavior is unwelcome.
2. Report it to your manager or People.
3. If the issue involves your manager, go directly to People or a founder.

All reports are taken seriously. Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is itself a violation of this policy.

## Consequences

Violations may result in a warning, a performance improvement plan, or termination, depending on severity and pattern.
