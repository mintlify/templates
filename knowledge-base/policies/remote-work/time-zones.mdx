Mintlify has team members across multiple time zones. Making this work requires intentional defaults.

## Async-first

Async-first means the default expectation is that people will respond when they're working, not immediately. A message in Slack is not an interruption that requires an instant reply.

If something is genuinely urgent and time-sensitive, say so explicitly ("need this before your EOD") or send a direct ping with context.

## Core overlap hours

Most Mintlify meetings happen during overlap hours across US time zones — roughly 10 AM – 3 PM PT / 1 PM – 6 PM ET. Check with your manager for your team's current meeting window.

Outside those hours, async is the default.

## Scheduling meetings

- Share your working hours in Google Calendar and your Slack status.
- When proposing a meeting time, offer options across time zones or use Calendly.
- Rotate meeting times if recurring calls are consistently inconvenient for some attendees.
- Record video meetings when possible so people who can't attend can catch up.

## Communication expectations

| Channel | Expected response time |
|---------|----------------------|
| Slack DM | Within your working day |
| Slack channel message | Within your working day |
| Email | Within 24 hours on a working day |
| Direct ping with "urgent" | As soon as reasonably possible |

Don't imply faster response is expected for non-urgent messages.

## Out-of-office and availability

Update your Slack status when you're heads-down, out sick, or on vacation. Set a Google Calendar out-of-office for absences over a day.

In a remote team, a quick status update prevents colleagues from wondering if something's wrong when you're quiet.

## Flexibility

Mintlify doesn't require fixed 9–5 hours. You're responsible for meeting your commitments and being available during core overlap hours for meetings. How you structure the rest of your day is up to you.
