When something breaks in production, the goal is to restore service as quickly as possible — then understand why.

## Severity levels

| Level | Description | Response target |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| SEV-1 | Docs sites down or data loss | Immediate, all hands |
| SEV-2 | Significant degradation, builds broken, major feature down | Within 30 minutes |
| SEV-3 | Minor degradation, workaround available | Within 2 hours |
| SEV-4 | Cosmetic or low-impact issue | Next business day |

## Responding to an incident

1. **Acknowledge** the alert in your alerting tool to stop re-paging.
2. **Assess severity** — SEV-1/2 or lower?
3. **Open a thread** — for SEV-1/2, start a thread in `#incidents` on Slack and tag the team.
4. **Mitigate first** — roll back on Vercel, disable a feature flag, or scale up before diagnosing root cause.
5. **Communicate** — post updates to `#incidents` every 15 minutes until resolved.
6. **Resolve and document** — mark the incident resolved and file a postmortem for SEV-1/2.
