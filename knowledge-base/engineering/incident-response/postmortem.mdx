title: "Postmortem process"
description: "How to write a blameless postmortem at Mintlify, facilitate the review, and make sure action items land."
---

Postmortems are required for all SEV-1 and SEV-2 incidents, and optional for recurring SEV-3 patterns. The goal is learning, not blame.

## Blameless means

Blameless means we focus on what failed in the system — processes, tooling, alerting, communication — not on who made a mistake. Engineers make good decisions with the information available at the time. The postmortem identifies where the system let them down.

## Timeline

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Within 24 hours of resolution | Create the postmortem doc in Notion from the template |
| Within 48 hours | Draft the timeline and contributing factors |
| Within 5 business days | Hold the postmortem review — async is fine for minor incidents |
| Within 2 weeks | Action items assigned and tracked in Linear |

## Postmortem template

Create a new Notion page using the "Postmortem" template (search "Postmortem template" in Notion). Fill in:

1. **Incident summary** — one paragraph: what happened, when, and the customer impact.
2. **Timeline** — chronological log of events in UTC. Include when the issue started, when it was detected, key actions taken, and when it was resolved.
3. **Root cause** — what was the underlying cause? Use "5 Whys" if helpful.
4. **Contributing factors** — what else made this worse or harder to detect?
5. **Impact** — number of customers affected, duration, any data involved.
6. **What went well** — things that helped during the response.
7. **Action items** — concrete, assigned to specific people, with due dates. At least one per significant contributing factor.

## The review

For major incidents (SEV-1), hold a synchronous 30-minute review. For SEV-2, an async review in Notion comments is acceptable.

Don't re-hash decisions made during the incident. Focus on what to do differently next time.

## Action items

Action items without owners don't get done. Every item needs:
- A clear description of what "done" looks like
- A single owner (not a team)
- A due date
- A Linear issue to track it

Founders review open postmortem action items periodically. If an item is blocked or stale, surface it in `#eng`.
